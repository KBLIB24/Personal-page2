My background

I have spent a large part of my adult life in education. First, I started out as a education student and earned by B.S. in education from EKU. While I was a student, I worked with small children in a pre-school setting. After graduation, I woked almost 9 years as a middle school science teacher. During that time I helped students build their science knowledge and their critical thinking skills. You can view examples of their work below.



Although I loved my content area and I loved teaching, I wanted to take time to explore some other options in education. So, after that, I transitioned into higher education. I currently work as an academic advisor, so I am still involved in education, and I have used my science skills to help other people interested in the field of science, particularly STEM studies and healthcare. While on this break from K-12 education, I began to research other types of positions in K12 that I might be interested in. The one that seemed to fit me the best was library media specialist. So, I returned to EKU to obtain my second masters degree and my Rank 1.
